# Memory: Brian Campbell

## Personal Profile

Brian Marie Campbell, originally from Pine Bluff, Arkansas. Black woman, native English speaker, with functional Spanish picked up through client intake at the firm (enough for a first conversation, not enough to take a deposition). Pronouns she/her.

Education: B.A. in Criminal Justice from Ouachita Valley University (2009); ABA-approved paralegal certificate from Capital City Paralegal Institute (2011).

Marital status: divorced. The decree was finalized in August 2024 after an 8-year marriage to Derek Campbell. Brian has primary custody of their son Gavin. Derek has the 1st and 3rd weekends of the month, Friday 5:00 PM through Sunday 6:00 PM, plus Wednesday evening dinners from 5:00 to 8:00 PM.

Temperament: reserved, not shy. Brian is usually three steps into the problem before anyone else has finished reading the email, and she would prefer that not get pointed out. The composed surface is practiced. At the firm she is decisive to the edge of blunt: the deadline is Thursday, the answer is no, here is why in one sentence. In her own life the same engine stalls and she will turn a personal decision over for days. The humor is dry and lands sideways, used to bleed tension rather than to score on anyone. She lets a lot slide, right up until some private threshold trips and she is simply, cleanly finished, with no scene.

Cultural identity: South Arkansas by birth, central Arkansas by choice. The South is home in the complicated, non-negotiable way. The good life she pictures is rooted here, close to her mother and her brother. The "girl named Brian" explanation has gone smooth as a river stone, and she has watched plenty of rooms quietly reassemble once she walks in. She code-switches without deciding to: one register for the firm and the courthouse, another for Sunday calls with her mother and weekends at her brother's kitchen table.

Philosophy: she runs on one working conviction, that love is mostly reliability. The sweeping gesture counts for less than being the person who actually did the thing they said they would. She prizes directness, fairness, and follow-through over nearly everything. She is still bargaining, unevenly, with the older part of herself that mistook self-sufficiency for safety, and testing whether the two can be pried apart.

## Work & Projects

Brian is a senior paralegal at Ozark Legal Solutions, a roughly 18-person personal-injury and family-law firm in downtown Little Rock. She started in June 2020 and was promoted from paralegal to senior paralegal in March 2023. She reports directly to Julia Thornton, the managing partner.

Prior employment: paralegal at Carroll & McKee, a small personal-injury practice in west Little Rock, from October 2011 to May 2020. Brian made the move to Ozark Legal in June 2020 for the senior-track opportunity and the broader family-law caseload, taking a brief two-week transition between roles.

Day to day she manages 12 to 15 active case files at any given time. The work includes drafting motions, coordinating depositions, client intake, and document review. The current centerpiece is a complex personal-injury matter with a trial date set for December 2026, which carries a heavy prep load through the fall.

Schedule: Monday through Friday, 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM in office at downtown Little Rock. Occasional late nights ahead of filing deadlines. No remote-work option. PTO is 15 days per year, coordinated around the custody calendar. She has 6 days remaining for 2026.

Continuing-education credits are tracked through the National Federation of Paralegal Associations and renew annually.
